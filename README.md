# AVIATION-WORKFORCE-HEALTH-STUDY
A data analysis project exploring BMI and health indicators among employees in an aviation company.

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the health metrics of aviation employees, focusing on BMI and other wellness indicators to identify trends that may impact employee well-being and workplace performance.

## Business Problem
Aviation organizations need to ensure their employees maintain good health and fitness because poor health conditions, such as high BMI and High Blood Pressure, can affect productivity, job performance and safety in aviation operations. However, there is limited analysis of employee health data to identify potential health risks within the workforce.

## Project Objective
To examine how employees' demographic details, job roles, and basic health indicators relate to their fitness status, and identify possible health risks that could affect safety and performance of employees.

## Dataset Description
The dataset used in this project contains health-related information collected from aviation employees. The dataset contains  twelve variables: Gender, Blood type, Fitness status, job role, Age, Height, weight, BMI, Blood Pressure Systolic, Blood pressure diastolic, Heart Rate, and vision score. 

## Methodology
- The dataset was provided by the course instructor and was already cleaned and prepared for analysis
- The dataset was examined to understand the variables and the overall structure of the data
- Additional columns such as BMI category and Age group were created to help categorize the data and make the analysis more meaningful
- Descripive statistics were used to analyze health inducators such as BMI and other relevant metrics
- charts and graphs were created to illustrate patterns and trends in the data
- The results were interpreted to idetify key insights related to aviation employee health

## Business Questions
- How does fitness status vary across different job roles?
- Do pilots differ significantly from other job roles in key health indicators such as heart rate, blood pressure, BMI, and Vision scores?
- Is there a significant relationship between BMI and Blood pressure levels among employees, and does this relationship differ by job role?
- How do age and fitness status interact to influence cardiovascular indicators (heart rate and blood pressure)?
- Are there noticeable differences in health indicators (BMI, Blood pressure, heart rate) between male and female employees within the same job roles?
